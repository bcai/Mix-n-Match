Gingerbread Cheesecake

by Leite's Culinaria / 1 comments

Time: 3 hr / Servings: 10

Ingredients

  • all-purpose flour (for dusting)
  • 1 gingerbread cookies (recipe unbaked)
  • 4 tbsps unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 lbs cream cheese (four 8-ounce packages, at room temperature
  • 1 and 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • 1/4 cup unsulphured molasses
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 and 1/2 tsps ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp grated nutmeg (freshly)
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 tsp grated lemon zest (finely, preferably organic, optional)

Directions

Make the Gingerbread Crust:

Preheat the oven to 350째F (177째C).

On a generously floured piece of parchment paper, roll 1/2 the gingerbread cookie dough to a 13-by-10-inch rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. Brush off any excess flour. Slide the parchment and dough onto a baking sheet and bake until the cookie is firm and golden brown, about 14 minutes. Transfer the parchment and cookie to a wire rack to cool completely. Roll, cut, and bake the remaining gingerbread cookie dough as directed in the Gingerbread Cookies recipe.

Break the single rectangular gingerbread cookie into large pieces and then pulse in a food processor until finely ground. Measure 2 cups gingerbread crumbs and dump them into a bowl. Reserve the remaining crumbs for another use, such as nibbling or sprinkling over ice cream. Add the butter and sugar to the crumbs in the bowl and mix until combined. Press the mixture firmly and evenly onto the bottom and 1/3 of the way up the sides of a 9-inch springform pan whose exterior has been wrapped with a double layer of aluminum foil. Bake the crust until set, about 10 minutes. Transfer the pan to a wire rack to cool completely.

Make the Cheesecake Filling:

Reduce the oven temperature to 325째F (163째C). Bring a small pot of water to a boil.

With a stand or handheld mixer on medium speed, beat the cream cheese until fluffy and smooth, about 3 minutes. Beat in the sugar and vanilla, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Reduce the speed to low. Add the eggs, 1 at a time, beating well after each addition and scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Beat in the molasses, salt, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and zest. Scrape the filling onto the cooled gingerbread crust.

Place the springform pan in a large, shallow roasting pan. Pull out the center oven rack and set the roasting pan on the rack. Carefully pour enough boiling water into the roasting pan to reach halfway up the side of the springform pan and carefully push the rack back into the oven. Bake the cheesecake until the filling is set but still slightly wobbly in the center and the surface is an even, gorgeous, pale shade of brown, 60 to 70 minutes. (We had the best luck smack at 65 minutes.) Transfer the springform pan to a wire rack to cool completely.

When the cheesecake has cooled to room temperature, refrigerate it, uncovered, for at least 8 hours and preferably overnight.

Before serving, run a knife around the edge of the gingerbread cheesecake to loosen the sides of the pan. Remove the sides. Arrange the gingerbread cookies in a circle in the center of the cake, if using.

  • For Gingerbread Lovers
    Submitted by Trudy Ngo-Brown on November 14, 2014 - 5:35

    If you love gingerbread, you have to make this gingerbread cheesecake recipe. The cookie dough is pretty standard and makes a tender cookie on its own. I included the lemon zest in the cheesecake filling, and I liked the little zip it added. I did think there was a tad too much nutmeg, and next time would cut back a little. The double layer of foil seemed to keep water out pretty well. My cheesecake baked for about 65 minutes, with the top turning a very pale brown and the edges slightly darker. It was really hard waiting overnight to taste this! The final product was smooth and not overly rich. Although I could have easily eaten the entire thing by myself, I gave half away to a friend and stored what was left, tightly wrapped, in the freezer. I cut myself a slice last night, and even after a week, the flavor and textures are still great. I might actually like it just as much frozen as I do at room temperature. I will definitely make this again and highly recommended it to gingerbread lovers!
